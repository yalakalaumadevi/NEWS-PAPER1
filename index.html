    • In a bid to provide financial assistance to meritorious students who dream of having an illustrious career, the government runs various schemes under which lakhs of students pursue their studies. For families who cannot afford the day-to-day expenses of their child’s education, these schemes prove to be of great help, eliminating all the hurdles on the way to their education. Since, the crucial time to make our career starts once we pass the Class 12 board exams, most of these scholarships focus on providing financial aid to students who are seeking enrolment under higher degree courses. Let’s take a look at the few schemes run by the government for students who have passed the Class 12 board exams: AICTE Pragati Scholarship

    PM Scholarship or Prime Minister Scholarship Scheme Under this scholarship scheme, the dependent wards and widows of Central Armed Police Forces & Assam Rifles (CAPFs & AR) and State Police Personnel are provided the help in pursuing their dream careers. Initiated in 2006-07, this scheme is run by the Ministry of Home Affairs. The Government of India recently approved some changes in the scholarship as it revised the scholarship amount for girls from Rs 2,250 to Rs 3,000 per month and from Rs 2,000 to Rs 2,500 per month for boys. KVPY Scheme KVPY stands for Kishore Vaigyanik Protsahan Yojna. It is a national fellowship scheme for students interested in pursuing research in basic sciences. Candidates are selected for the scheme through a test that is conducted annually by the Indian Institute of Sciences (IISc) Bangalore. Those studying in the 11th or 12th grade are eligible for the scheme. Besides, the ones in the first year of any undergraduate course in basic science are also eligible for this scheme.
